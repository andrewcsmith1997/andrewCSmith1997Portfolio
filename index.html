Intro

I’m a data analyst skilled in SQL, Excel, Tableau, and Python. I’m excellent at extracting and manipulating specific information from CSV files, SQL databases and APIs for different business purposes, and using visualization models, dashboards, jupyter notebooks, and slide decks to accurately present my findings. I’m passionate about data analysis because I enjoy thinking outside the box, coming up with new ideas, and sparking conversation. Check out my work.

Work

NY Retail Food Store Inspections - 'Find Store Records' Dashboard and 'County Analysis' Jupyter Notebook
Used: Python (Pandas), SQL (JOIN, Aggregate Functions), Excel, Tableau
  • The data was collected from data.ny.gov , posted by New York's Department of Agriculture and Markets’ Division of Food Safety and Inspection.
  • Built ETL Pipelines using Python (Pandas) and SQL Queries.
  • Authored an article explaining inspection guidelines and instructions navigating dashboard.
  • Conducted two sample t-tests to see if there was correlation between New York counties and the number of critical deficiencies found during an inspection.
  • Constructed data visualizations and dashboards in Tableau to pair with recommendations in Article and Jupyter Notebook
Vehicle Fuel Economy - Model and Slide Deck
Used: Excel (Index/Match, Booleans, Pivot Tables)
  • The data was collected from Kaggle , but was originally created by the US Department of Energy and US Environmental Protection Agency.
  • Developed a model in Excel that conducts two sample t-tests to find the statistical impact of three different car features on a car’s vehicle fuel economy and presented my findings in a slide deck.
  • Used pivot tables, descriptive statistics, Excel's Data Analysis ToolPak, and automated all of the statistical analysis calculations in the model for easy user interaction.
  • Resulted in a system that is designed to improve a company’s ability to recommend a vehicle to a customer based off the customer’s preference.
Improving Airline Passenger Satisfaction - Jupyter Notebook
Used: Python (Pandas), Tableau, Jupyter Notebook (Google Colab)
  • The data was collected from Kaggle.
  • Conducted an A/B test using Python that takes in a passenger variable and automatically generates two samples determined by mean score, test results, and statistical significance to see the proportion of passengers who are satisfied/not satisfied with their flight.
  • Generated data frames for the A/B test using Pandas, created data visualizations in Tableau, and created a Jupyter Notebook in Google Colab to present my findings.
  • Discovered that online boarding is a significant determining variable for passenger satisfaction.
NAEP SQL Queries - Jupyter Notebook
Used: SQL (JOIN, HAVING, Subquery), Python (Pandas, SQLAlchemy), Jupyter Notebook (Google Colab)
  • Accessed a PostgreSQL database in Python using SQLAlchemy and executed SQL queries to extract and manipulate data from a table that contained student test scores for US states.
  • Generated data frames for the data pulled from the queries using Pandas and presented my results in a Jupyter Notebook made through Google Colab.

About

I believe being a Data Analyst was always my calling; I just didn't realize it until only recently. During the latter half of college, I would be asked what I wanted to do or who I wanted to be, but never had an answer I could confidently say. Approaching graduation, I still wasn't too sure, but settled on IT work/systems management due to my background working with technology. I got a contract job doing IT work for a small manufacturing company and studied for a CCNA certification exam in my spare time. After doing this for a few months, I reached a point where I wasn't enjoying the work I was doing and felt I needed to re-evaluate what I wanted to do with my career. I took some time to reflect and figure out what was right for me. My experiences from school and work went into my thought process, but it was understanding what I loved to do in my free time that had the biggest influence. I realized that I had always been passionate about being the most creative person in the room and coming up with ideas that no one’s thought of before.
Fast forward to today and I’m now a data analyst who has the motivation, hard skills, and tools necessary to pursue my passion.
